    A Lesson from the Fish

    A beautiful day snorkeling at Honolua Bay turns scary when my sister goes missing in the water, reminding us how quickly fun can turn into fear and why family always has to stick together.

    The trees swayed as we walked through the narrow trail, the hot air sticking to me like glue. Sweat slid down my back, and my arms were already sore from carrying my fins and mask. Every step felt harder than the last.

    “Take some pictures of me here. I’ll take some of you after,” my sister said, already posing with one hand on her hip.

    I snapped a few.

    “No, no, the angle’s wrong. Take it higher,” she criticized.

    “Seriously? I’ve taken like a million already. Just take some of me now,” I whined.

    “Girls, are you just gonna spend the whole time taking photos? We’re not even at the bay yet. Hurry up,” my dad groaned, definitely questioning his life choices.

    We kept walking. The smell of saltwater got stronger, like the ocean was yelling, almost there. Finally, we stepped out of the trees, and there it was — Honolua Bay.

    It was the type of place people put onto postcards. The bright blue water sparkled like diamonds, the black rocks sizzled under the sun, and the trees rocked peacefully. I could hear small waves slapping against the rocks and people nearby jumping into the water.

    “Let's go,” Dad said, practically sprinting to the shore.

    The rocks were burning hot, like walking on a pan, but weirdly I kind of liked it. It felt better than the muddy trail. My sister and I started putting on our gear.

    “I hope this new snorkeling gear actually works,” she said, already suspicious.

    “Yeah, water better not leak into my eyes and mouth,” I joked.

    I plunged into the water, and the coldness seeped into my body like a thousand needles at once. It made me feel alive after hiking in the hot sun. Dad cannonballed in right after, splashing my sister so badly she shrieked.

    “Ahh my eyes are burning,” she yelled.

    “You’re fine. Just get in already!” I laughed, wiping water from my face.

    Finally, she slid into the water, still complaining. But once we started snorkeling, it was amazing. Large schools of fish zipped past me, all staying together. An octopus quickly curled into the rocks, trying to hide. Then I heard crunching. Chomp Chomp! My sister pointed at a red parrotfish gnawing on coral. It sounded exactly like bubble wrap popping, echoing strangely through the water.

    Then she suddenly shot up. I slowly followed her to the surface.

    “There’s water in my mask and snorkel,” she coughed.

    I waited patiently while she fixed it, but it kept happening.

    “This is so annoying. I’m going back to the shore,” she said.

    “I’ll go with you,” I said.

    We made it back to the mossy rock we always used. It was slimy and slippery, like soap. She climbed on to fix her gear.

    Dad waved me over. “Let’s swim while she fixes it. No point in waiting,” he said.

    So I followed him. He was kicking like a motorboat, leaving bubbles everywhere, and I ended up drifting behind. We got carried away and didn’t even realize how far we’d gone until we turned back and the rock was empty. My heart dropped

    “She’s not there,” I said, panicking.

    We scanned the water. I thought I saw her yellow fins, but the bathing suit was wrong. Dad and I started yelling her name, which echoed across the bay. People started staring.

    “We should split up,” I said.

    “No. You stay here, I’ll check the rocks,” Dad ordered.

    Stay here? Seriously? That’s the worst plan ever. She’s out there somewhere, and I’m just floating here being useless. I could be swimming around and looking for her right now. But then I looked at his worried face, watching his eyes darting everywhere. He wasn’t just worried about her. He was worried about me too. I floated there, shivering as the wind hit my wet skin. What if she drowned? I should’ve stayed with her. My brain was spiraling.

    Dad tried climbing on the mossy rock to get a better view, but in a rush he slipped. BAM!He fell right back into the water. I lost it, laughing so hard I couldn’t breathe. People stared like, wow, some daughter and sister you are. But I couldn’t help it, that’s just how I deal with stress — I laugh at the worst times.

    Meanwhile, Dad was panicking, asking strangers, “Have you seen my daughter?”

    His voice shook, and suddenly it hit me. What if she wasn’t just nearby? The water all looked the same, and my heart pounded harder the longer we didn’t see her.

    Finally, after what felt like forever, I saw a familiar head bobbing in the water. Relief slammed into me.

    “OMG, where did you go,” I yelled.

    “I saw you guys swim out, so I tried to catch up,” she said, like it wasn’t a big deal.

    Dad swam over and hugged her tight.

    “I was worried sick about you. Why would you just leave?” he yelled.

    A fisherman scowled. “Keep it down. This is supposed to be a peaceful place.”

    Dad went red. “Sorry. I couldn’t find my daughter.”

    My sister rolled her eyes. “Why’d you guys go so far? You said you’d stay close.”

    “We didn’t realize how fair we swam out. I should’ve stayed with you,” I admitted.

    Dad pulled us into a soggy group hug. For a moment, Honolua Bay didn’t feel like paradise. It felt like a reminder. The ocean is beautiful, but things can turn from fun to frightening easily. That day, snorkeling wasn’t really about the fish. It was about us. Just like fish stay in schools to protect each other, we had to stick together, and watch out for one another. Getting lost is only funny once everyone makes it back safely. Still, the funniest part will always be Dad slipping on that slimy rock.

    The LOL Doll Incident

    A simple day out with my mom turns stressful when my new doll almost rolls into a pond, teaching me to be more careful.

    “Mom, can we go out today?”I asked. “Maybe another day since your sister is out with her friends and she won't be able to come,” mom responded.

    No, that's not fair!

    “But that's not fair because I’m not going out with my friends,” I said angrily.

    “Ok fine, but just this once without your sister,” mom reluctantly agreed.

    Yes, mom is the best!

    My mom and I went into our dark metallic blue car and drove off. We decided that we would go to a Thai restaurant since that was our favorite. After lunch my mom checked her pockets, and found coupons for CVS. So we decided to go there and get a couple of things. Once we arrived I browsed through the items closely following my mom in fear of getting lost. My mom stopped at the makeup section and looked at all of the makeup which felt like hours. So I decided that I would venture off in the CVS.

    “Mom, can I get some toys or candy?” I asked.

    I really hope that mom lets me get something because if she does this day will get even better!

    “Yeah, sure but make sure you don’t go too far.” Mom responded.

    Mom is the absolute best!

    I quickly ran out of the makeup aisle in excitement to look for some candy, or toys. My first stop was the candy aisle. I continuously turned around to make sure my mom was in the same place I left her. After I had checked that she was still there I looked at all of the candy scanning to find my favorite lollipops. But unfortunately, they weren’t there so I ran to the toy aisle. I look at all of the stuffed animals wondering about who would ever get them. When I spotted it, a bright green ball that had the letters “LOL Doll”. The moment I saw it, I quickly grabbed it. I ran over to my mom in excitement and asked her if I could get it.

    “Mom, Mom!,” I screeched in excitement, “Can I get this doll?”

    “How much is it?” Mom asked.

    Hopefully mom still lets me get this doll even though it's quite expensive.

    “Eleven dollars,” I responded.

    “Ok, but you better play with it since it's very expensive,” mom said.

    Thank goodness mom let me get this doll. If she didn't I would’ve been upset for the rest of the day!

    We then checked out and paid for our items. My mom decided that we would go to a park that had lots of ponds and geese so that I could open my new doll there.

    I’m so excited to go to the park because I love ponds and animals!

    After we arrived at the park we found a clean bench that we could sit at and open the toy. My mom took out her phone and started recording me opening the doll. I started peeling off the plastic from the ball acting like the YouTuber’s I used to watch. Everything was going to plan until I reached the middle of the ball and pulled too hard on the plastic and lost grip of the ball.

    Oh no! Mom is going to be so mad!

    The ball started rolling fast in the direction of the pond. It seemed like it was going as fast as a raging bull! My mom and I both ran after it and fell down on the cement. My mom stretched her arm and grabbed the ball at the last moment. I started crying because I thought that my legs were bleeding since I fell down.

    “I’m so sorry,” I cried.

    “I shouldn’t have ever asked you to get me this doll,” I continued, “I’ll never ask you for anything again.

    “It’s ok you don’t need to cry, it was just a mistake and I was still able to catch it,” mom said.

    I kept my legs away from my mom because I was afraid to see what they looked like after I had fallen. But my mom looked at my legs and they were fine. But my mom had a scrape on her knee and I felt so bad.

    If only I hadn't asked for the doll, mom wouldn’t have gotten hurt. It’s all my fault! That should have been me who got hurt since I was the one who wanted the doll.

    After that we went back home and I continued to open my doll while my mom treated her scrape. After I took off all the plastic I saw my doll, she had a green dress and pink hair. I was quite disappointed.

    I wanted the glittery doll. But I won’t complain to mom about the doll because it has already caused her to get hurt.

    In the end, even though my mom scraped her knee because of my LOL doll and I didn’t get the doll I wanted, I still had an amazing day and I learned that I should be more careful with new things

    The Dangling Spider

    Dinner takes a dramatic turn when I think my sister is pranking me about a spider, only to find out she was telling the truth.

    “Mom, I’m hungry,” I said. “Tanisha, dinner is almost ready,” my mom responded. Soon, my mom put food in front of me and my sister. I picked up some vegetables with my fork and started eating. I was enjoying my dinner because I hadn’t eaten a lot during the day. I loved feeling my stomach getting filled with warm delicious food.

    Suddenly, I saw my sister staring at me with an expression of disgust and fear. “What?” I asked my sister. “Tanisha, there is a spider next to you,” she responded. I knew that she was pranking me because she has done pranks like these in the past. And I decided that I wasn’t going to fall for them any longer. But my gut kind of felt like she was telling the truth because of the expression on her face. I knew that she was probably faking it and she just got better at acting. “I know that you’re joking, cut the act already,” I told my sister with the voice of annoyance. “I’m not joking! Seriously just look to your right.” My sister responded. After a couple of minutes, my mom walked out of the office room and into the room my sister and I were sitting. My mom screamed, “There is a spider next to you, Tanisha, don’t move.” I had a feeling that my mom was also in on the prank so I decided to ignore her. By that time, after all of this spider drama my food became cold. So I got off my chair carrying my food. I decided since I wasn’t on my chair anymore, I could just look to my right to see if there was actually a spider. As I turned my head 180 degrees to the right I saw a white spider connect to a cobweb as long as the Eiffel Tower! “Ahhh,” I screamed. “See I told you there was a spider,” my sister said. I quickly got up from my chair and ran straight to my dad.

    “Papa, there is a spider next to my chair. Can you put it outside?” I asked my dad. After asking my dad and telling him that the spider will take over our house, he reluctantly got off his working chair. “It’s just a small spider, why can’t you put it outside?” He asked. My dad gently put the spider into a paper cup and took it to our front yard. I followed him as he held the cup. As my dad cautiously put the cup down in a slanted way the spider quickly scurried away. “Don‘t ever think about coming back, Mr.Spider,” I said laughing. Just by looking at the spider running away with its long skinny legs just made my skin crawl. I then went back inside and saw my mom and sister. “Sorry for not believing you guys,” I muttered. “It’s okay, I mean I do play a lot of pranks on you,” my sister replied. After that incident I always look both ways before sitting down on my chair.

    Getting Pushed by the Waves

    A relaxing day at the hotel pool and beach turns intense when a huge wave knocks us over, but it becomes one of those moments my family still laughs about.

    It was my fourth day in Hawaii and all I wanted to do was to go to my hotel’s swimming pool and to the ocean that’s in front of it. I convinced my family that we should stay at the hotel’s property so that we’re not so tired out like the other couple of days. And they reluctantly agreed. We decided that we’d first go to the pool then the ocean. My dad, sister, and I swam in the pool and went on the water slides while my mom just stayed in the pool. We were in the pool for quite some time so we decided to move on to the ocean.

    My sister and I grabbed our floaties and headed to the ocean. My sister and I liked to sit on the floaties and move with the ocean’s current rather than swimming. The big waves started coming so my mom and dad kept a close look on my sister and I. They were also in the water. The sky started forming dark gray clouds all over the beach. My dad’s legs were covered with wounds because while he was walking in the ocean’s water he stepped on a couple of sharp rocks.

    Then a ginormous wave crashed on everyone who was in the ocean. My sister and I toppled into the ocean. Though the water wasn’t very deep I jumped out of the water and jumped onto my dad because I was scared of drowning. Once I calmed down I got back onto my floatie. My family and I stayed at the beach for the rest of the day joking about what had happened. Still to this day my family and I laugh about this incident.

    Snorkeling in Hawaii

    A fun day at the pool in Hawaii turns into drinking a bunch of pool water, but it ends with me finally learning how to snorkel and learning some patience too.

    It was my second day in Hawaii after landing in the airport. That day my family and I decided that it was time to go to the pool in my hotel. We brought our snorkeling gear just in case we decided to go snorkeling in the ocean. We also brought our floaties in case we changed our minds.

    Once we arrived at the pool my sister and I headed straight for the water slides. We each took our turn and went down to the water slide. My parents then decided that it was time to head to the ocean and go snorkeling. But the thing was I forgot how to snorkel. So my sister decided that she would teach me in the swimming pool. She first put on my gear then her’s. But I felt like she was taking too long to put on her gear and I just wanted to go snorkeling. So I decided that I would just do it myself. I went a bit too deep into the pool water and I tried to breathe through the mouth piece. But if you have gone snorkeling before then you know that if you breathe through the mouthpiece underwater you’re going to end up drinking the pool water. So that’s what exactly happened. When I breathed in, all of the water went into my mouth. But before I understood what happened I accidentally swallowed the water. I gagged after I accidentally swallowed the water because the pool water tasted terrible.“Eww,” I screamed. “That’s what happens when you're not patient,” my sister exclaimed.

    After my sister taught me how to snorkel. And then my family and I went to the ocean to go snorkeling. At the end of the day I learned that I need to be more patient when waiting for something. So whenever I’m impatient I go back to this moment and try to figure out what I could do instead of being impatient.

    First Time Walking Back Home

    Walking home alone for the first time feels terrifying at first, but after crossing busy streets and facing fears, it becomes a moment of independence I will never forget.

    “Bye Ms.Ou see you tomorrow!” I excitedly said. It was my first time walking back home by myself. I felt like there were butterflies in my stomach. “It’s ok you can do this, just run back home,” I told myself nervously. I walked through the park. I saw so many other kids playing, one on the swings and another on the rock wall climbing. I was super nervous, “it’s okay just keep going, you can do this,” I confidently said to myself.

    I ran and ran until I made it to the first crosswalk, when I thought to myself, “I can’t run forever,” I tried to pace my breath. Then I looked to my left then to the right to make sure no cars were there. But I was scared. What if a car comes out of nowhere? So yes, I stood there for five minutes and no car ever came! I finally crossed making sure to constantly look to both sides.

    Then I finally made it to the second crosswalk. It was practically burning, I felt like I was melting like a snowman! But that was when the REAL PROBLEM came along. When I was about to go I saw a yellow car with a man inside of it driving. I told him to go. But then he was telling me to go. But then I was telling him to go. “Oh no, I’m going to be here forever!” I groaned to myself. It was like the man and I were arguing about who was going to go first, until I finally decided to go. While I was going, I remembered what my parents told me, “make sure to always make eye contact with the driver so he still knows that you're there.” So I did exactly what my parents told me to do, and I gave that man a serious look as I walked away.

    Finally, I saw my house. I got ready to run and I went off. By the time I reached my house my face was all red and I was completely out of breath. Just when my mom asked, “How’d you get here so fast, and why does it look like you just ran a marathon?” “Uhm it does haha, well I mean our house is pretty near the school and I probably have fast legs!” I replied. My mom looked at me knowing clearly I was lying to her.

    Going to Florida

    A family vacation starts with travel chaos and a hotel fire alarm scare, but turns into one of the most fun cruise trips ever.

    I was walking home from school, and I finally reached my house. Today was an exciting day because I was going on vacation! My whole family was home, we were finishing up packing. At 7:00 pm my dad called an uber to take us to the airport, but my dad did not look at the car the uber driver was driving in, and when the uber driver arrived we tried to fit the suitcases inside but there was no space. So my dad called another uber driver, but this car had enough space. So it took us about 30 minutes to reach the airport. We bought some food. At 9:00 pm we boarded the plane. At 4:00 am we finally reached Florida! We were going on a cruise but that was in 2 days. On the second day at 5:00 am in the morning there was a fire alarm, and 5 minutes before the fire alarm my sister was feeling hot and she couldn't sleep. When the fire alarm started my sister jumped right out of the bed and took all the phones. My mom got up and was taking all the suitcases and my dad was putting on shoes. And I was sleeping. I didn't even hear the fire alarm so my sister had to wake me up! We all rushed down the five floors and when we got to the lobby they said that there was no fire and someone was just smoking. We were all awake in the hotel room and none of us could go back to sleep. We were a little scared about the cruise now. But at 9:00 am we were at the ship platform and we were entering the cruise. We first went inside our room and then we went to the buffet for lunch. We were on the cruise for a week! It was so fun. My sister and I went on the water slides about 30 times at least! After the cruise we were in Florida for one more week then we had to come back home! My sister and I didn't want to come back and our parents also didn’t want to. Luckily, we had one more day and then school would start again.The End!

    The Day School Closed

    Spring break was supposed to be one extra week, but it ended up changing school for the rest of the year.

    “Wow this movie is just like the book!” Tanisha said. Tanisha’s class was watching the movie Winn Dixie because they finished reading the book. Unfortunately, Tanisha’s 3rd grade teacher wasn’t there to watch because she got sick. After Tanisha came back home from an exciting day at school she was stunned to hear the spring break was extended. “Spring break is going to be an extra week long!” Tanisha said in shock. “Yeah! I got to come back home early from school today!” Replied Tanisha’s older sister. “No fair!” said Tanisha. Tanisha’s mom printed three thick stacks of paper; one for ELA, one for math, and one for science. “Are you kidding me! Why do I need to do homework during spring break?” Tanisha said in anger. “Well you need to do something,” Tanisha’s mom replied. From that day forward Tanisha had to do a couple of pages of homework every single day. Soon came the end of extended spring break. “Noooooo! I don’t want to go back to school,” Tanisha groaned. “Guess it's your lucky day! School is closed for the rest of the school year.” From that day school was online and Tanisha only met with her class every Thursday at 10 am for 30 minutes.
