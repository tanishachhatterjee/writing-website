    The Promise of the Waterfall

    In a desert village that used to live in peace, everything falls apart when food and water start running out and people turn against each other. With the help of a goddess and Mother Nature, the villagers have one last chance to fix their mistakes and learn how to live in harmony again.

    There once was a peaceful tribe living in the dry desert. Nobody ever fought. Until one day, when all the humans and animals started depleting all of the natural resources. Once they barely had any food and water left over, the villagers started becoming greedy and started to steal from others. It got so bad that the humans were breaking the village, and some of the humans had left the village. The once peaceful village now turned into a village with angry villagers.

    One day, a goddess named Darla came down from heaven to try and help the humans. She tried to persuade the humans who left to come back. She tried to tell the humans who did have food to share with the others. But everything she did didn’t help fix the problem. After some time, Darla just couldn’t help fix the problem, so she called Mother Nature to see if she could place a treaty between the villagers.

    Once Mother Nature came down from heaven she went one by one to all of the villagers' broken houses and asked them to meet her at noon when the sun sets. Soon it was noon and all of the villagers gathered around the center of the village. Some looked so lanky that it seemed like they hadn’t eaten or drank water for a few weeks. They immediately started shouting and screaming at each other. Soon they were all once again fighting with each other. Mother Nature and Darla tried their best to keep them contained from hurting each other.

    Finally Mother Nature said in a loud strong voice, “In order for me to give all of you more resources for food and water, you must all take an oath that you will never fight again.” Soon everyone was signing a piece of tiger skin that was going to be hung in the center of the town to show that the villagers signed an oath to be peaceful again.

    Once after all, the villagers signed the oath. Mother Nature and Darla controlled the weather to make it rain. Soon all of the dry and dead grass started turning into a luscious green color. Soon there were ponds and lakes. Most of all Mother Nature and Darla created a small mountain with a beautiful magical waterfall that will grant every wish anyone asks for. Darla taught the villagers how to take care of their new natural resources.

    Mother Nature put a treaty between the villagers, while Darla brought the villagers who left, back to the village. Soon the village went back to its peaceful self. And the villagers took great care of their new resources so they never ran out of food and water again.

  • Short Fantasy Story

    Why Do Giraffes Have Long Necks?

    When some giraffes start teasing an elephant at the watering hole, they don’t expect the elephant king to step in. After a powerful curse changes them forever, the giraffes learn a lesson they will never forget — and their long necks prove it.

    Deep, deep down in the Safari Forest lie giraffes and elephants. One day, a giraffe went to get some water from a lake. The giraffe saw many other giraffes, and there was one elephant. The giraffes mocked and smirked, and the elephant was laughing meanly.

    One of the giraffes said, “Hey, you loser, what do you think you're doing here with your long nose? This is our lake, not yours!”

    Another giraffe meanly agreed, smirked, and said, “Yeah, what do you think you're doing here, elephant?”

    The elephant responded in a crying voice, “What do you mean? We’ve shared this lake for years. Have you all forgotten, and why are you all teasing me about my trunk? I thought we were friends?”

    Slowly, other elephants came out.

    Thunk, thunk, it was the elephant king. He said, “You’re being mean to the wrong elephant. You shall all be cursed! Wha haha!”

    Then these wizard-looking, sacred elephants showed up, and they chanted, “Long necks mixed with sadness, curse these mean giraffes!”

    Slowly, all of the giraffes had long necks, and they were about the same size as the trees!

    After that, the giraffes knew not to mess around with elephants. Now that’s the story of why giraffes have long necks!

  • Realistic Fiction

    Little Bobby

    On his first day of school, Bobby is told he is too small to play basketball and he feels hurt and embarrassed. But at his teacher’s wedding, Bobby’s small size becomes the very thing that saves the day and proves everyone wrong.

    "Squeak, squeak," said the little chick. It was his first day of school. The chick's name was Bobby. His teacher's name was Ms. Chan. During recess, Bobby wanted to play basketball. He asked the other chicks if he could join them. Jonny said that Bobby was too small to play, and all the other chicks nodded.

    "I am not," said Bobby.

    "Okay, then try throwing the ball in the hoop. You only get three tries," said Jonny.

    "Okay," said Bobby.

    The first try, Bobby did not get it in. The second try, Bobby did not get it in. And on the third try, Bobby also did not get it in.

    "Hahahaha," said all the boys.

    "I don't care what you think," said Bobby as one tear rolled down from his face.

    When his mom picked him up from school, she asked, "What's wrong, chickidydoo?"

    "I am too short to play basketball," said Bobby.

    "No, you're not," Bobby's mom said.

    "Now who said that?"

    "Jonny and his friends," cried Bobby.

    "Well, tell them they're wrong," said his mom.

    "I did, but they didn't listen," said Bobby.

    "Oh," said Bobby's mom. "Why don't you play with someone else?"

    "I don't know," said Bobby.

    "Well, you can think about it later after you do your homework," said Bobby's mom.

    "Okay," said Bobby.

    The next day, after Bobby's mom dropped Bobby off, she got a letter from Ms. Chan saying she was getting married, and she invited Bobby and Bobby's mom to come. After Bobby's mom picked up Bobby, she told him the great news. Of course, Bobby was happy for Ms. Chan, except Jonny and his friends were going to be there too. Bobby's mom went to the wedding store. She bought a dress for herself and bought Bobby a suit. Two weeks later, Bobby's mom was getting ready with her dress, and Bobby was getting ready with his suit. You probably know where Bobby's mom and Bobby are going. To Ms. Chan's wedding!!!

    The ceremony started, and Jonny was walking with the rings.

    "Bammm!!"

    The rings went flying inside the piano, and Jonny had tripped.

    "Nooo," said Ms. Chan.

    "I am super duper sorry, Ms. Chan," said Jonny.

    "It's alright. I am pretty sure someone can put their hand inside the piano and get out the rings," said Ms. Chan.

    "Who has the smallest hands?" one of Jonny's friends said.

    "Bobby does!" said Jonny.

    "Bobby, will you please take the rings out of the piano?" said Ms. Chan.

    "Yes, I will," said Bobby very proudly.

    Bobby put his hand inside the piano. When he took his hand out, he had both of the rings.

    "Hooray," said everyone.

    "Yay, Bobby saved the day," said Jonny and his friends.

    After the marriage was over, Jonny and his friends came over to Bobby, and they all said sorry. They said that they were very rude the other day, so they wanted to apologize, and they also told Bobby he could play basketball whenever he liked with them. After that, Bobby never had any more problems.
